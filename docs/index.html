Hey, i'm Teddy.

I am an electrical engineering student at the Columbia University.

Since 15, I have explored the intersection of hardware and software. I specialize in embedded systems, analog and digital circuit design, and microcontroller programming.

Outside of development, you can find me playing guitar, wandering around lower Manhattan, or in the gym.

Beyond reading datasheets and applying Ohm's law, I've built up an intuive understanding of circuits from years on hands-on tinkering.

I pride myself on a genuine passion for electrical engineering and the satisfaction of bringing ideas to life through circuit design and implementation.
